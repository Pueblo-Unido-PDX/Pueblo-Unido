Get Help

For detention or removal cases

At Risk of Deportation?


If you or someone you know is currently detained or facing removal proceedings in the NW, please contact us. We help people navigate immigration legal services, advocate for effective representation, and support evidence-gathering to strengthen cases against deportation.

Contact us via phone at: 503-912-8925. Email: pueblounidopdx@gmail.com

Why Legal Representation Matters


If someone you know is detained and would like legal aid with their removal defense, they can:

  1. Hire a private immigration attorney to represent them;
    • NWIRP List of Removal Defense Attorneys in WA
    • AILA Immigration Lawyer Search by State
    • Catholic Charities Oregon Immigration Legal Resource Referral List 2016: English | Spanish
    • Immigration Attorney Referral List 2017 (OR)
    • If someone you know does hire an attorney, it is important that they know their rights as a client and the attorney’s responsibilities to them. The State Bar Association offers several resources to help clients find licensed attorneys, review their disciplinary history, and advocate for their rights as clients.
    • If you would like support in searching for a removal defense attorney, contact us at 503-912-8925 or pueblounidopdx@gmail.com
  2. Request that a nonprofit legal service provider take on their case at a reduced cost (compared to private representation);
    • Catholic Charities
    • Immigration Counseling Service
    • Lutheran Community Services
    • SOAR
    • These Portland-based nonprofits may/might not take cases for people detained at the Northwest Detention Center in Tacoma, WA, and may take only a limited number of cases for people detained at the Northern Oregon Regional Correctional facility (NORCOR)
  3. Seek informational materials created for pro se respondents.
    • Pro se respondents are those that represent themselves in immigration court. By definition, pro se respondents do not have legal counsel/representation.
    • If you/someone you know is detained and unable to get legal assistance from a private attorney or a nonprofit legal service organization, you/they can search for resources for pro se respondents (which are intended to be informational materials, and do not substitute for legal representation).
    • If someone you know is detained at the NW Detention Center in Tacoma, WA and is representing themself in their removal proceedings:
      • The NW Immigrant Rights Project is a non-profit legal service organization that offers comprehensive informational materials to assist pro se respondents who are detained at the NWDC.
      • People detained at the NWDC can call NWIRP toll-free at: 1-877-814-6444 to leave a message or schedule a visit with an NWIRP rep/attorney
      • Please note, while NWIRP works hard to visit every person in detention, they are not able to represent individuals in court or fill out relief applications/petitions on respondents’ behalf.
      • NWIRP also developed these helpful guides for detained immigrants and their families:
        • Guide to the NW Detention Center in Tacoma / Una Guia al Centro de Detencion en Tacoma
        • ICE’s instructions regarding NWDC procedures:
          • Instructions from Seattle ICE Field Office regarding NWDC detention procedures (Enforcement and Removal Leadership, visitation info for all types of visitors, FOIA requests, finding status of immigration case, submit complaints about mistreatment/misconduct by ICE or contract services employee, local procedures e.g. posting bond, sending mail, sending money)
          • For info about posting a departure or delivery bond (for someone detained for removal at the NW Detention Center in Tacoma, WA), click here.
          • ICE Detention and Reporting Information Hotline in English: 1-888-351-4024
            • English flyer
          • Línea de Información y Denuncias de los Centros de Detención
            • Spanish flyer
  4. If you know a pro se respondent in detention and neither a private attorney nor a nonprofit legal service provider is able to support them:
    • The Florence Immigrant and Refugee Project offers a wide range of informational materials about how to defend your own case/Como defender su propio caso
      • “These self-help materials are designed for detainees to use in representing themselves when applying for some common forms of legal relief from removal. Please note they are written for detainees in Arizona and most were last updated in 2013, with support from the American College of Trial Lawyers Emil Gumpert Award.”
      • “These publications are meant to provide useful basic information about immigration law and detention in removal in Arizona and were created in the course of the Florence Project’s work. Under no circumstances do they constitute legal advice.”

If someone you know is a pro se respondent detained at a facility in Oregon:

  • Immigration Counseling Service offers reduced-cost legal services on a case-by-case basis, and may be able to help.
    • To request a consultation with ICS, download this consultation request form, fill it out to the best of your ability and mail or email it to ICS
    • If you would like support from Pueblo Unido in filling out and turning in your consultation request form, contact us at (see top of page for contact info).
    • Please note: ICS is a non-profit legal service organization based in Portland, OR. ICS can only take on a limited number of detention cases in Oregon, and cannot support detention cases in other states.
